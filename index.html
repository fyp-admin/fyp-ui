Where do Space Explorations stand today??

Launched by the Soviet Union (predecessor to Roscosmos) in 1957, Sputnik 1 became the first artificial satellite to orbit Earth. This history-making event shattered the boundaries of human achievement, ushering in the Space Age and igniting a fierce competition between the US and the Soviet Union for space exploration supremacy. Sputnik's success not only spurred advancements in rocketry and satellite technology but also captured the world's imagination, forever altering our perception of what was possible beyond our planet.

What's more in the Universe out there??

Dwarf Planet Disguised as an Asteroid: Once classified as the largest asteroid, Ceres was reclassified as a dwarf planet in 2006. Unlike most asteroids, which are irregular in shape, Ceres boasts a nearly spherical shape, suggesting it may have undergone internal geological processes similar to planets. It holds the distinction of being the only known celestial body in the asteroid belt, located between Mars and Jupiter, that is both dwarf planet and the largest object.
